# Research Question

## Primary Research Question

To what extent does the absence of digital trade infrastructure and AI-enabled market intelligence exclude smallholder agricultural producers in Central Asia from participating in global commodity markets, and how might conversational AI platforms redefine the customer engagement model between producers and international buyers in the region?

## Sub-Questions

1. What structural and informational barriers prevent smallholder commodity producers in Turkmenistan, Uzbekistan, and Kazakhstan from accessing international agricultural markets directly?

2. How do information asymmetries between local producers and multinational trading intermediaries manifest in price outcomes at the farm gate, and what is the quantifiable margin gap?

3. To what extent can AI-powered matching algorithms, real-time price intelligence, and automated document generation replicate or replace the functions currently performed by intermediary trading chains in Central Asian agricultural export markets?

4. What language, literacy, and digital infrastructure constraints must be addressed for AI-enabled trade platforms to achieve adoption among smallholder producers in low-connectivity environments?

5. How do existing models of AI-mediated B2B trade (e.g., Alibaba's Rural Taobao, Agriledger, Twiga Foods) translate to the regulatory, linguistic, and infrastructural context of Central Asia?

## Academic Domain

- Agricultural Economics
- Digital Trade & Market Access
- Human-Computer Interaction (Low-Literacy / Multilingual Contexts)
- AI Ethics & Fairness in Emerging Market Platforms

## Suggested Methodology

Mixed-methods: quantitative analysis of commodity price data (farm gate vs. international spot) combined with qualitative interviews with producers, cooperative directors, and trading intermediaries across the Central Asian Agricultural Corridor.
