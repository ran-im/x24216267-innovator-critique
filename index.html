The Problem

"A farmer in Mary, Turkmenistan earns $0.28/kg for export-grade peanuts. The same cargo lands in Hamburg at $1.40. The 78% gap is not about quality. It is about access."

Central Asia sits at the geographic heart of the world's oldest trade network. The Silk Road once made this region the switchboard of global commerce. Today, 180,000 smallholder producers operate in near-total market opacity — no pricing data, no verified buyer networks, no export documentation support.

Intermediaries exploit this information gap, capturing 55–70% of the margins that belong to the people who grew the crop.
